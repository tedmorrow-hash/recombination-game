Decoding the Genome

In EvoSim, "fitness" is simple. Each organism has a genome of 1s and 0s. Fitness is the percentage of '1's. A higher percentage means a better-adapted individual, with a greater chance to survive and reproduce.

High Fitness (90%)

1110111101

This organism is well-suited to its environment and is very likely to pass on its genes.

Low Fitness (20%)

0100001000

This organism is struggling. It's a prime candidate for being culled by selection pressure.

The Generation Cycle

Each "generation" in the simulation is a complete loop of life, death, and reproduction. Here is the step-by-step process that repeats continuously.

1. Reproduction

Parents are chosen to create offspring, shuffling genes based on the Recombination Rate.

↓

2. New Offspring

A new generation is born, with a mix of old and new genetic combinations.

↓

3. Selection

The environment applies its pressure, culling the least-fit individuals from the new generation.

↓

4. Survivors

The remaining individuals form the parent pool for the next generation. The cycle repeats.
